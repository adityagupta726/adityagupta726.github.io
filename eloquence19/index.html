Group Discussion
"Believe that words are strong , they can overwhelm what we fear when fear seems more awful than life is good."
"Group Discussion" is a way to express and evolve. This happens everyday with us be it in train or bus or in classroom or college canteen with both strangers and friends so who not win buy doing nothing but just expressing your views.

1. Teams will be announced in the beginning (5-10 members).
2. The preliminary round will be theme based. One member of the team will pick up a chit and a random theme will be selected.

4. 30 seconds will be given to the participants to gather their thoughts and prepare their content.
5. Teams will be given 10 minutes to discuss the given topic.
6. Any kind of misbehavior or indecency will result in disqualification.
7. In case of any discrepancy, the decision of the judge(s) will be final and binding.
8. Participants must report 1 hour prior to their event/slot.
9. All participants must carry their valid college ID cards.

Just A Minute
For every minute you remain angry you give up sixty seconds of peace of mind.
We welcome you to take that anger out of your words. BPIT PRESENTS YOU JUST -A -MINUTE (English), a coordination of mind and mouth. Make it through 60 seconds and take away the reward.
Can you make it through sixty seconds of non-stop talking without hesitation, repetition, or deviation? Or will the sheer pressure make you crumble and have your competitors pounce on you?
The participants will be divided into random groups in which they have to compete against each other and gain maximum points.
Get your creative strategies out!


Rules:

1. No questions or arguments once the final decision has been made by the JAM master.
2. The fellow competitors can interrupt or object the speaker wherever they find the need to. The JAM master decides to what extent it is justified.
3. You get a penalty if the competition objects on any of the following counts:
3.1. You object out of turn.
3.2. You stutter, stammer or even indulge in any “time-wasting tactics”. These include efforts to buy time like interspersing your talk with “uhh”, “aah” etc.
3.3. Early start or late start.
3.4. Speaking too fast or too slow.
3.5. Grammatical errors.
3.6. Undue stress on words or emphasis in the sentences.
3.7. Repetition of a previously used word.
3.8. Not following any “random rule” as set by the JAM master.
4. The total time is sixty seconds for each group exclusive of the time used up during objections.
5. Points are given in the following manner:
5.1. Positive points for every second that you speak.
5.2. Positive points for every correct objection.
5.3. Negative points for every incorrect objection.
5.4. Bonus points for being the first and the last speaker.
6. The one with maximum points at the end of rounds will win.
7. In case of any discrepancies, the decision of judges and organizing committee shall be considered final.
8. Participants must report 1 hour prior to their event.
9. All participants must carry their valid college ID cards.
